{{glanceSubhead}}

{{title}} is a one-acre garden parcel made of two adjoining freehold plots, each measuring approximately twenty-one by eighty metres and laid side by side to form a clean, rectangular acre. The land sits off Rosslyn Close in northern Nairobi, screened from the road by mature trees and softened underfoot by years of careful, low-key planting — heliconia, bamboo, traveller's palm, indigenous canopy.

It is offered as a single assemblage. The two titles can be developed jointly as one estate residence with generous setbacks, or kept as a pair — one to live on, one to hold. Either way, the work of waiting for a Nairobi garden to grow has already been done.

§ II — The Address

{{locationHeadline}}

Rosslyn sits on Nairobi's northern ridge — a long-established diplomatic and residential pocket bordering Karura Forest, ten minutes from the UN complex at Gigiri and a short drive from the village schools, embassies and cafés that anchor daily life in this part of the city. The parcel sits at the One Off Gallery, off {{title}}.

The neighbourhood is one of Nairobi's quietest at this scale — large garden plots, embassies and ambassadorial residences, with through-traffic kept to Limuru Road and UN Avenue. The eucalyptus and cape chestnut canopy that defines Rosslyn carries straight onto the parcel; the air, even mid-afternoon, is noticeably cooler than the city below.
